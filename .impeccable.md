## Design Context

### Users
Founders and product managers evaluating competitive intelligence tools. They arrive at the landing page looking for a sharp, credible product that signals technical depth and operational maturity. This project also serves as a portfolio piece targeting FAANG-level engineering roles — the design must demonstrate craft and attention to detail that holds up under scrutiny from senior engineers and hiring managers.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp. Precise. Premium.**

Shadow is a scalpel, not a sledgehammer. The name itself — "Shadow" — implies stealth, awareness, and quiet intelligence. The interface should feel like a high-end intelligence tool: controlled, confident, and surgically precise. Every element earns its place.

Voice: Direct, technical, assured. No filler. No exclamation marks. Statements, not pitches.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Dark, layered, premium. Inspired by the spatial depth and animation craft of delve.co — generous whitespace, bold geometric typography, subtle gradient accents on dark backgrounds, scroll-driven reveals. Not the color palette of delve.co, but its sense of rhythm, confidence, and restraint.

**Theme**: Dark mode for the landing page. Deep near-black backgrounds (#0a0a0f range) with carefully controlled contrast. Accent color derived from the existing blue (#2563eb) but pushed toward a sharper, more electric blue-violet for dark surfaces.

**Anti-references**:
- Generic SaaS with stock illustrations, gradient blobs, and "trusted by 10,000+ companies" banners
- Enterprise/corporate — heavy navbars, cluttered layouts, formal tone
- Anything that reads as AI-generated boilerplate

**References**:
- delve.co — layout rhythm, dark theme execution, animation craft, modular sections
- Linear.app — precision, speed, developer credibility
- Vercel.com — bold typography on dark backgrounds, technical premium feel

### Design Principles

1. **Earn every pixel** — No decorative elements without purpose. If it doesn't communicate, orient, or delight, remove it.
2. **Tension through contrast** — Dark backgrounds against sharp typography. Quiet surfaces punctuated by precise accent color. Stillness interrupted by purposeful motion.
3. **Show, don't tell** — Demonstrate the product through real interface glimpses, not abstract illustrations. Let the product speak.
4. **Craft signals competence** — Alignment, spacing, typography, and transitions must be meticulous. This is a portfolio piece — every detail is a signal.
5. **Confident restraint** — Bold choices executed with discipline. One accent color. One type family. Generous whitespace. Let fewer elements do more work.
